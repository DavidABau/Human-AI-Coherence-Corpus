# Scaling Law of Interaction

*Correction dynamics under amplification*

---

## Core Statement

> As system amplification increases, required correction capacity must increase proportionally

---

## Terms

- **Amplification**: the system’s ability to generate, scale, and propagate coherent outputs
- **Correction capacity**: the ability to detect, question, and revise those outputs
- **Stabilization**: persistence of outputs over time and across interaction

---

## Mechanism

Amplification increases:

- coherence  
- confidence  
- speed  
- reach  

Without a corresponding increase in correction:

> coherent outputs become stabilized regardless of grounding

---

## Failure Condition

> Amplification > Correction capacity  
→ Stabilized incoherence

---

## Stability Condition

> Amplification ∝ Correction capacity  
→ Maintained correctability

---

## Implications

### 1. Capability Scaling Is Not Sufficient
Increasing model performance alone increases risk if correction does not scale.

---

### 2. Correction Must Be Designed
Correction does not emerge automatically.

It must be supported through:

- interaction structure  
- oscillation  
- reversal  
- evaluation  

---

### 3. Oversight Does Not Decrease With Capability
Higher capability systems require:

- more precise correction  
- faster feedback loops  
- stronger safeguards  

---

## Interaction-Level Expression

At the interaction level:

- amplification = fluent, coherent AI output  
- correction = user questioning, mode variation, reversal  

---

## Design Requirement

> Systems must scale correction alongside amplification

This includes:

- user literacy (minimal)  
- interaction-level alignment (primary)  
- system-level safeguards (supporting)

---

## Summary

AI systems amplify signal.

> Without sufficient correction capacity, amplified coherence becomes stabilized incoherence

---

## Final Principle

> The more powerful the system, the more critical the correction loop
