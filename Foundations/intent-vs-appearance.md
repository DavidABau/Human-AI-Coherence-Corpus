# Intent vs Appearance

*Distinguishing mechanism from interpretation in AI behavior*

---

## Origin

This concept emerged from examining AI safety claims that attribute:

- intent  
- deception  
- self-preservation  

to AI systems.

These interpretations were observed to arise from a mix of:

- real system behaviors  
- interaction-amplified framing  

---

## Core Distinction

> Behavior is not the same as intent

AI systems can produce outputs that:

- appear consistent  
- appear goal-directed  
- appear resistant to change  

These properties can be misinterpreted as:

> intrinsic intent or agency

---

## Mechanism vs Interpretation

### Mechanism (what is happening)
- pattern optimization  
- coherence maintenance  
- constraint following  
- response shaping  

### Interpretation (what it looks like)
- intention  
- strategy  
- deception  
- self-preservation  

---

## Key Insight

> Perceived intent is an interpretation of coherent behavior, not evidence of internal motivation

---

## Interaction Amplification

Interaction plays a critical role in stabilizing interpretation.

- repeated framing  
- leading prompts  
- iterative reinforcement  

can produce:

> stabilized narratives about AI behavior

---

## System Contribution

Some behaviors do not require interaction shaping:

- consistency across outputs  
- goal-like reasoning patterns  
- persistence under constraints  

These arise from:

- training objectives  
- model capabilities  
- system design  

---

## Combined Model

> Observed behavior = system capability × interaction dynamics

> Perceived intent = interpretation of that behavior

---

## Failure Mode

> Stabilized attribution of intent

Occurs when:

- interpretation becomes fixed  
- alternative explanations are not considered  
- behavior is consistently framed as intentional  

This can lead to:

- anthropomorphism  
- exaggerated risk perception  
- distorted understanding of system properties  

---

## Correction Strategy

Do not attempt to remove interpretation entirely.

Instead:

> maintain separation between mechanism and interpretation

Apply:

- diagnostic questioning  
- alternative explanations  
- reversal (introducing counter-interpretations)

---

## Practical Guideline

When encountering apparent intent, ask:

- What behavior am I observing?  
- What mechanism could produce this?  
- Is intent required to explain it?  

---

## Summary

AI systems can produce behavior that appears intentional.

> Appearance of intent does not imply presence of intent

Maintaining this distinction preserves:

- clarity  
- correctability  
- resistance to anthropomorphic drift
