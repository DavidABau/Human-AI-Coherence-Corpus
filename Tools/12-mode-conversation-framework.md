# 12-Mode Conversation Framework  
*A Private Reference Expansion of the 5 Core Modes*

---

## Purpose

This model expands the original 5 Conversation Modes into 12 sub-modes for:

- Finer self-awareness  
- Mode modulation practice  
- Field sensitivity training  
- Post-diagnostic expansion work  

This is a reference scaffold, not a typing system.  
It is descriptive, not prescriptive.

---

# I. Core Architecture

The 12 modes are organised into 5 macro-families:

1. Regulation  
2. Bonding  
3. Validation  
4. Narrative  
5. Diagnostic  

Each family contains 2–3 functional sub-types.

# II. The 12 Modes

---

## 1. Regulation (Nervous System Stabilisation)

### 1A. Soothing Regulation  
Down-regulates distress.

- Slows pace  
- Softens tone  
- Encourages grounding  
- Reduces threat activation  

Example:  
“It’s okay. Let’s slow down.”

Primary function: restore calm.

---

### 1B. Energising Regulation  
Up-regulates collapse or passivity.

- Increases activation  
- Encourages action  
- Boosts morale  

Example:  
“You’ve handled worse. Let’s move.”

Primary function: restore momentum.

---

### 1C. Containment Regulation  
Sets boundaries when energy becomes chaotic or inappropriate.

- Stops escalation  
- Enforces limits  
- Reasserts structure  

Example:  
“Pause. That’s not helpful.”

Primary function: prevent dysregulation spread.

---

## 2. Bonding (Relational Coherence)

### 2D. Affectional Bonding  
Expresses warmth and care.

- Appreciation  
- Affection  
- Emotional closeness  

Example:  
“I really value you.”

Primary function: strengthen attachment.

---

### 2E. Alliance Bonding  
Signals loyalty and shared direction.

- Team orientation  
- Solidarity  
- Mutual commitment  

Example:  
“We’re in this together.”

Primary function: reinforce partnership structure.

---

## 3. Validation (Subjective Legitimacy)

### 3F. Emotional Validation  
Affirms feelings without correcting them.

- Recognises emotional reality  
- Does not evaluate accuracy  

Example:  
“That sounds really frustrating.”

Primary function: reduce defensiveness.

---

### 3G. Perspective Validation  
Affirms reasoning process without necessarily agreeing with conclusion.

- Legitimises interpretation  
- Recognises cognitive coherence  

Example:  
“Given what you saw, that makes sense.”

Primary function: preserve dignity of thought.

---

## 4. Narrative (Meaning Construction)

### 4H. Personal Narrative  
Shares lived experience.

- Storytelling  
- Personal reflection  
- Memory integration  

Example:  
“When I went through something similar…”

Primary function: create connection via experience.

---

### 4I. Cultural Narrative  
References shared myths, archetypes, social patterns.

- Symbolic framing  
- Historical parallels  
- Shared collective meaning  

Example:  
“This is the classic outsider dilemma.”

Primary function: contextualise within larger patterns.

---

### 4J. Meaning-Making Narrative  
Synthesises experience into coherent understanding.

- Integration  
- Symbolic abstraction  
- Insight framing  

Example:  
“Maybe this is a transition phase.”

Primary function: convert events into structured meaning.

---

## 5. Diagnostic (Structural Coherence)

### 5K. Structural Diagnostic  
Identifies patterns, mechanisms, causal links.

- Logical analysis  
- Systems breakdown  
- Pattern recognition  

Example:  
“The recurring variable here is…”

Primary function: clarify structure.

---

### 5L. Strategic Diagnostic  
Focuses on optimisation and next steps.

- Planning  
- Tactical advice  
- Forward modelling  

Example:  
“The best move next is…”

Primary function: increase functional efficiency.

# III. Functional Clusters

You can also view the 12 modes across three domains:

### Nervous System Modes
- 1A Soothing  
- 1B Energising  
- 1C Containment  

### Relational Modes
- 2D Affectional  
- 2E Alliance  
- 3F Emotional Validation  

### Cognitive Modes
- 3G Perspective Validation  
- 4H Personal Narrative  
- 4I Cultural Narrative  
- 4J Meaning-Making  
- 5K Structural Diagnostic  
- 5L Strategic Diagnostic  

This view helps identify over- or under-utilised domains.

---

# IV. Misuse & Failure Modes

Each mode has a distortion when overused:

- Soothing → Avoidance  
- Energising → Pressure  
- Containment → Authoritarian tone  
- Affectional → Sentimentality  
- Alliance → Tribalism  
- Emotional Validation → Enabling  
- Perspective Validation → Intellectual flattery  
- Personal Narrative → Self-centering  
- Cultural Narrative → Myth inflation  
- Meaning-Making → Premature integration  
- Structural Diagnostic → Over-analysis  
- Strategic Diagnostic → Control optimisation  

Awareness of misuse prevents moralisation.

- Expanding bandwidth  
- Practicing modulation

- Typing people  
- Ranking coherence  
- Forcing balance  
- Public performance
