# Failure Pattern Recognition

This tool helps you identify common breakdown patterns in thinking and conversation.

These patterns are not moral failures.  
They are structural failures in how signal is processed.

Recognising them early prevents escalation, confusion, and false certainty.

---

## Failure Patterns

Recognise these patterns early to prevent escalation.

![Failures](https://github.com/DavidABau/Human-AI-Coherence-Corpus/blob/main/Tools/failure-patterns.png)

---

## 1. Signal Overload

**What it is:**  
Too much input, too many variables, or too much intensity for the system to process coherently.

**What it looks like:**
- jumping between points
- loss of clarity
- fragmented or unfinished thoughts
- rising confusion or fatigue

**What’s happening:**
The system cannot stabilise because the signal exceeds processing capacity.

**What to do:**
- reduce scope
- isolate one issue at a time
- slow the pace
- clarify inputs before proceeding

---

## 2. Premature Stabilization

**What it is:**  
Locking onto an answer, interpretation, or conclusion before it has been properly tested.

**What it looks like:**
- “That must be it”
- quick agreement without depth
- resistance to further questioning
- fragile certainty

**What’s happening:**
The system stabilises early to reduce uncertainty, not because coherence has been achieved.

**What to do:**
- re-open the question
- introduce alternative perspectives
- test assumptions
- ask: “What might we be missing?”

---

## 3. Stabilized Incoherence

**What it is:**  
A pattern that is internally stable but misaligned with reality.

**What it looks like:**
- confident but incorrect conclusions
- repeating the same explanation despite contradictions
- resistance to correction
- reinforcing feedback loops

**What’s happening:**
The system has formed a stable attractor around a distorted or incomplete signal.

**What to do:**
- introduce external reference points
- reset the frame
- step outside the current explanation
- prioritise reality over internal consistency

---

## 4. Mode Misalignment

**What it is:**  
Using the wrong interaction mode for the situation.

**What it looks like:**
- analysing when someone needs validation
- agreeing when clarity is required
- avoiding when engagement is needed
- escalating when regulation is needed

**What’s happening:**
The response does not match the signal type, causing friction or breakdown.

**What to do:**
- identify the current signal (emotional, unclear, unstable, relational)
- switch to the appropriate mode
- adjust pace and tone accordingly

---

## Key Principle

Breakdowns are rarely random.

They follow recognisable patterns.

Once you can see the pattern, you can change the outcome.

---

## Reminder

- Not all stability is coherence  
- Not all agreement is resolution  
- Not all confidence is accuracy  

Coherence requires alignment with reality, not just internal consistency.
