# Tool: Detecting Visible vs Private Relationship Drift

## Purpose

To help identify when a relationship is drifting out of shared awareness  
and prevent “sudden” breakdowns caused by unshared internal processing.

---

## The Core Check

Ask:

> “Are we processing this together, or am I processing it alone?”

If the answer is consistently “alone,” drift may be forming.

---

## 1. Self-Check (Internal Drift)

Use this when reflecting on your own state.

### Early Signals
- “It’s probably not worth bringing up”
- “I’ll just figure this out myself”
- “I already know how this will go”
- Decreasing desire to discuss certain topics

### Mid Signals
- You have a **clear internal view** the other person hasn’t heard
- You feel **less need to include them**
- You are adjusting expectations **without discussion**

### Late Signals
- You feel emotionally “done” or detached
- You are making decisions **independently of the relationship**
- You are no longer trying to repair or resolve

---

## 2. Other-Person Check (Hidden Drift)

Use this to observe shifts in the other person.

### Early Signals
- Less sharing of internal thoughts or feelings
- Conversations become more surface-level
- Fewer attempts to resolve issues together

### Mid Signals
- Reduced emotional reactivity (appears calmer, but flatter)
- Stops raising concerns they previously cared about
- More withdrawal during meaningful conversations

### Late Signals
- Minimal engagement beyond logistics
- Lack of curiosity about the relationship
- Sudden decisions that seem “out of nowhere”

---

## 3. Relationship-Level Check

Ask:

- Are important topics being **co-processed**, or avoided?
- Are concerns being raised **early**, or only after they are formed?
- Does either person have **unshared conclusions** about the relationship?

If yes:

> The relationship may be partially operating in a private layer

---

## 4. Quick Intervention (Keep It Simple)

You do not need full clarity. Just reopen the loop.

### Use early, low-pressure signals:
- “This might be nothing, but I want to flag it early”
- “I’m still thinking this through, but something feels off”
- “Can we look at this together before I form a view?”

### If the other person is withdrawing:
- “No pressure to solve it now, but I want to stay connected on it”
- “We can take space, just don’t want it to disappear completely”

---

## 5. What *Not* To Do

- Don’t wait until you are certain before speaking
- Don’t assume silence = no problem
- Don’t force deep conversation under pressure
- Don’t interpret calmness as resolution

---

## 6. Key Principle

> Shared reality must be maintained in real time

If internal processing is not periodically externalised:

> The relationship can drift without either person realising

---

## Summary

- Drift begins with **unshared processing**
- It grows through **lack of co-processing**
- It becomes visible only when **decisions are already made**

Prevention is simple:

> Externalise earlier than feels necessary
