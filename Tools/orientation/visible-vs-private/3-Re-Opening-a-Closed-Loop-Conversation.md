# Tool: Re-Opening a Closed Loop Conversation

## Purpose

To gently reintroduce shared processing when a conversation or relationship  
has shifted into internal (closed-loop) processing.

---

## When to Use

- The other person has withdrawn or gone quiet  
- Conversations feel surface-level or incomplete  
- You sense something unresolved but unspoken  
- You realise you’ve formed views without sharing them  

---

## The Core Principle

> Do not force resolution — restore shared awareness

The goal is not to “fix” the issue immediately, but to:

> Re-open the channel for co-processing

---

## 1. Recognise the State

Closed-loop indicators:

- Silence or withdrawal under pressure  
- Avoidance of meaningful topics  
- Internal conclusions without discussion  
- Repeated “we’ll talk later” that never happens  

---

## 2. Choose the Right Entry (Low Pressure)

Avoid intensity. Start light and open.

### Examples

- “Hey, I don’t think we ever really closed the loop on that”
- “This isn’t urgent, but I’d like to come back to something when you’re ready”
- “I might be off, but it felt like something got left hanging”

---

## 3. Lower the Stakes Explicitly

Make it safe to engage without pressure.

- “We don’t have to solve it now”
- “I’m not trying to push anything”
- “Just want to keep it open rather than leave it sitting”

---

## 4. Invite, Don’t Pull

Give them control over engagement.

- “Whenever you feel up to it”
- “Happy to leave it for now if you need space”
- “Just didn’t want it to disappear completely”

---

## 5. Share Lightly (Re-open Your Side)

Offer a partial signal, not a full conclusion.

- “I realised I’ve been thinking about it a bit”
- “Something about it didn’t sit quite right with me”
- “No solid view yet, just wanted to bring it back into the open”

---

## 6. If They Engage

Shift into co-processing:

- Listen first  
- Keep responses simple  
- Avoid jumping to conclusions or solutions  

Focus:

> Understanding before resolution

---

## 7. If They Don’t Engage

Do not escalate.

Instead:

- Acknowledge and step back  
  - “All good, we can leave it for now”
- Keep the channel open  
  - “Just didn’t want it to get lost completely”

---

## 8. What *Not* To Do

- Don’t demand answers (“we need to talk now”)  
- Don’t present fully formed conclusions  
- Don’t interpret withdrawal as rejection  
- Don’t overload with emotion or analysis  

---

## 9. Key Pattern

Closed loop:

> Internal processing → silence → distance

Re-opened loop:

> Signal → safety → shared awareness → optional processing

---

## 10. Simple Template

Use when unsure:

> “This isn’t urgent, but I felt like something got left unresolved.  
> No pressure to deal with it now — just wanted to keep it open.”

---

## Summary

- Re-opening is about **access**, not resolution  
- Safety and timing matter more than content  
- Light signals are more effective than heavy ones  

> The goal is not to solve the problem  
> It is to make the problem **shareable again**
