# Tool: Recognising a Fully Closed System

## Purpose

To identify when attempts to re-open shared processing are no longer effective,  
and to prevent getting stuck in repeated, one-sided efforts to reconnect.

---

## The Core Principle

> Not all closed loops can be re-opened

A system is fully closed when:

> Internal decisions are complete  
> and the person is no longer available for co-processing

---

## 1. Key Indicators (Consistent Pattern Required)

### Communication Pattern
- No meaningful engagement over time
- Responses are minimal, delayed, or purely practical
- Avoidance of any deeper or relational topics

### Emotional Pattern
- No visible curiosity, concern, or investment
- Flat or neutral tone regardless of context
- No response to attempts at connection

### Behavioural Pattern
- Maintains distance consistently
- Does not initiate contact
- Does not follow up on previously open topics

---

## 2. Response to Re-Opening Attempts

You have already:

- Approached calmly  
- Lowered pressure  
- Invited without forcing  
- Left space for timing  

And the result is:

- No engagement  
- Repeated deflection  
- Continued silence  

---

## 3. The Shift to Recognise

Move from:

> “How do I get this back into the open?”

to:

> “This is no longer a shared space”

---

## 4. Common Misinterpretations

Avoid these traps:

- “They just need more time”
- “I need to say it better”
- “If I stay patient, it will reopen”

These can keep you in a loop when:

> The other person has already exited internally

---

## 5. Emotional Reality

This stage often includes:

- Confusion (“How did it get here?”)
- Hope loops (“Maybe one more try”)
- Self-doubt (“Did I do something wrong?”)

These are normal, but not reliable indicators of reversibility.

---

## 6. What to Do Instead

### 1. Stop Active Re-Opening Attempts
- Do not continue initiating the same conversation
- Avoid escalating intensity or frequency

### 2. Accept the Boundary
- Recognise lack of engagement as information
- Treat silence or distance as a clear signal

### 3. Re-Centre Yourself
- Shift focus from the relationship → your own stability
- Re-engage with external supports (friends, routines, structure)

### 4. Keep Communication Clean (if needed)
- Practical, respectful, non-emotional
- No pressure, no probing

---

## 7. What *Not* To Do

- Don’t chase or pursue repeatedly  
- Don’t try to “break through” withdrawal  
- Don’t over-explain or over-analyse  
- Don’t interpret disengagement as a puzzle to solve  

---

## 8. Key Distinction

Re-openable system:

> Some responsiveness + some uncertainty

Fully closed system:

> Consistent non-engagement + stable distance

---

## 9. Final Check

Ask:

- Have I given this multiple low-pressure opportunities?
- Has there been any meaningful engagement in return?
- Am I the only one trying to keep this open?

If yes:

> Treat the system as closed

---

## Summary

- A closed system is not a failure to communicate  
- It is a **lack of mutual participation**

> When shared processing is no longer possible,  
> the only stable move is to stop trying to force it

---

## One-Line Orientation

> “If it can’t be shared, it can’t be repaired”
