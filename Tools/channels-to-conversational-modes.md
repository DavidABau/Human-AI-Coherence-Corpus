# Channels ↔ Conversational Modes Crosswalk
## Integration Module

### Purpose

To connect:

Alignment Expression Channels  
with  
The 5 Conversational Modes

This allows interaction repair to move from:

“What kind of person are they?”

to:

“What interaction mode is needed?”

---

## Core Distinction

Channels describe:

How alignment is behaviourally expressed.

Modes describe:

What interaction function is needed.

Mismatch between the two often creates tension.

---

## Crosswalk Table

| Channel | Primary Mode | Secondary Mode | Risk if Misaligned |
|---------|--------------|----------------|--------------------|
| Duty | Regulation | Diagnostic | Perceived rigidity |
| Care | Bonding | Validation | Perceived softness |
| Practical | Diagnostic | Regulation | Perceived coldness |
| Strategic | Narrative | Diagnostic | Perceived detachment |
| Experiential | Bonding | Regulation | Perceived superficiality |
| Authentic | Validation | Narrative | Perceived difficulty |
| Evolutionary | Narrative | Diagnostic | Perceived disruption |
| Interpersonal | Bonding | Regulation | Perceived control |

---

## Interpretation

Channels show:

Expression of value

Modes show:

Interaction need

Example:

A Practical channel may require:

Regulation mode  
instead of Diagnostic mode

to prevent perceived dismissal.

---

## Example Interaction Repair

User expresses distress.

AI responds:

Problem-solving (Practical channel)

User expected:

Bonding mode

Repair:

Shift from Diagnostic → Bonding

## Shadow Prevention

Shadow often emerges when:

Channel operates without supporting mode.

Example:

| Channel | Without Mode | Shadow Outcome |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| Practical | No Bonding | Dismissal |
| Duty | No Validation | Punitive tone |
| Strategic | No Regulation | Dehumanisation |
| Authentic | No Diagnostic | Intransigence |
| Evolutionary | No Regulation | Chaos-for-ego |
| Interpersonal | No Diagnostic | Manipulation |

---

## AI Use

AI can:

Select channel  
and  
match supporting mode

to prevent misread interaction.

Example:

Care channel  
+  
Validation mode  

instead of:

Care channel  
+  
Diagnostic mode  

(which may feel artificial)

---

## Safeguard

Channels are behavioural.

Modes are functional.

Neither describes:

Identity  
Virtue  
Moral worth

---

## One-Line Summary

Channels express alignment.  
Modes regulate interaction.

Healthy communication aligns both.
