# Interaction Modes (Quick Guide)

This tool helps you choose *how* to respond in a conversation.

Most conversational breakdowns are mode mismatches, not disagreement

Different situations require different modes.  
Using the wrong mode often creates confusion, tension, or breakdown.

---

## The Five Modes

### 🧯 Regulation
**Use when:** the signal is overwhelmed, tense, or unstable  
**Goal:** stabilise the system

**What it looks like:**
- slowing things down
- pausing escalation
- creating space

**Common mistake:** avoiding the issue entirely

---

### 🤝 Validation
**Use when:** the input is emotional or personally significant  
**Goal:** preserve signal integrity

**What it looks like:**
- acknowledging the other person’s experience
- reflecting back what you heard
- showing understanding without necessarily agreeing

**Common mistake:** blind agreement or loss of clarity

---

### 🔍 Diagnostic
**Use when:** something is unclear, contradictory, or confusing  
**Goal:** reveal structure

**What it looks like:**
- asking precise questions
- identifying assumptions
- separating issues

**Common mistake:** coming across as cold, dismissive, or overly analytical

---

### 🧩 Narrative
**Use when:** meaning needs to be formed or integrated  
**Goal:** organise experience into something understandable

**What it looks like:**
- summarising patterns
- connecting ideas
- framing the situation

**Common mistake:** over-interpreting or adding unnecessary meaning

---

### 🔗 Bonding
**Use when:** the relationship itself needs attention  
**Goal:** maintain connection

**What it looks like:**
- warmth
- humour
- shared experience
- presence

**Common mistake:** avoiding clarity or difficult topics

---

## Quick Selection Guide

If the signal is unstable → **Regulation**  
If the signal is emotional → **Validation**  
If the signal is unclear → **Diagnostic**  
If the signal needs meaning → **Narrative**  
If the relationship matters → **Bonding**

---

## Mode Misalignment (Common Failure)

Many conversations break down not because of disagreement,  
but because the wrong mode is being used.

Examples:

- Using **Diagnostic** when someone needs **Validation**  
- Using **Bonding** when clarity is required  
- Using **Regulation** to avoid necessary discussion  

---

## Key Principle

Modes are not identities.

You are not a “diagnostic person” or a “validation person.”  
You are selecting the mode that best supports **coherence in the moment**.

---

## Reminder

- Stabilise before analysing  
- Validate before correcting  
- Clarify before concluding
