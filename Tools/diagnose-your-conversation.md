# Diagnose Your Conversation

This tool helps you quickly identify where a conversation is breaking down, and what to do next.

No prior knowledge of HACC is required.

---

## 1. What’s happening?

Select the closest match:

- [ ] The conversation feels confusing or messy  
- [ ] We’re going in circles  
- [ ] Something feels “off” or misaligned  
- [ ] The conversation is escalating or tense  
- [ ] We seem to agree, but nothing is actually resolving  

---

## 2. Where is it breaking?

### Confusing or messy  
→ **Raw Signal Issue**  
Input is fragmented, unclear, or overloaded.

---

### Going in circles  
→ **Coherence Loop Issue**  
The system is not resolving contradictions or aligning structure.

---

### Feels “off”  
→ **Relational Misalignment**  
Signals are being interpreted differently across participants.

---

### Escalating or tense  
→ **Signal Weighting Failure**  
Emotional intensity is overpowering structure and clarity.

---

### Agreement without resolution  
→ **Premature Stabilization**  
The system has locked onto a pattern before it is fully coherent.

---

## 3. What to do

### Raw Signal Issue
- Slow down
- Clarify inputs
- Separate distinct points
- Reduce scope

---

### Coherence Loop Issue
- Identify contradictions
- Compare assumptions explicitly
- Ask: “What are we each actually saying?”

---

### Relational Misalignment
- Restate the other person’s point in your own words
- Check for shared reference
- Align on definitions

---

### Signal Weighting Failure
- Reduce emotional intensity
- Switch to regulation or validation mode
- Pause if needed

---

### Premature Stabilization
- Re-open the question
- Introduce alternative perspectives
- Test the current conclusion

---

## Key Principle

You are not trying to “win” the conversation.

You are restoring **signal coherence** so that understanding becomes possible.

---

## Reminder

- Clarity precedes agreement  
- Structure precedes meaning  
- Coherence precedes resolution
