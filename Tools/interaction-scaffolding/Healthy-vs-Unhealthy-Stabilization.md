## Healthy vs Unhealthy Stabilization

Stabilization is necessary for coherence, but becomes dangerous when it suppresses correction.

Healthy stabilization:
- Maintains coherence and stability
- Preserves openness to contradiction
- Allows ongoing correction and refinement

Unhealthy stabilization (stabilized incoherence):
- Maintains coherence and stability
- Suppresses contradiction
- Replaces correction with reinforcement

The key difference is not coherence, but whether correction remains possible.
