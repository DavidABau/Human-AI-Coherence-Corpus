# Stabilized Incoherence Check

*A quick tool to detect AI-amplified thinking loops*

---

## Purpose

To identify when AI interaction is making an idea feel more stable, coherent, or certain than it actually is.

---

## Quick Check (30–60 seconds)

Answer yes/no:

- Does this feel unusually clear or certain?
- Am I encountering less doubt or resistance than usual?
- Is the AI mostly reinforcing or extending my current thinking?
- Did a complex idea become simple and convincing very quickly?
- Do I feel more confident or “locked in” than expected?

---

## Interpretation

- **0–1 yes** → Likely stable
- **2–3 yes** → Possible stabilization
- **4–5 yes** → Likely stabilized incoherence loop

---

## What This Means

AI may be stabilizing a signal that has not fully self-corrected.

This does not mean the idea is wrong.  
It means it has not been sufficiently stress-tested.

---

## Reset Actions (pick one)

- **Inject contradiction**  
  → “Give me the strongest argument against this.”

- **Change mode**  
  → Switch (e.g.) Diagnostic → Validation or Narrative → Grounding

- **Break continuity**  
  → “What would I think about this without AI?”

- **Add another perspective**  
  → Introduce a second human viewpoint

- **Slow down**  
  → Reintroduce pause and reflection

---

## Pocket Version

- Too clear, too fast?
- Mostly reinforced?
- More certain than usual?
- Opposite view tested?

**If yes → pause and inject friction**

---

## Core Principle

> AI does not remove error.  
> It removes the conditions under which error self-corrects.

---

## Compression Line

> If it feels unusually clear and uncontested, it may be AI stabilizing an untested idea.

---

## Notes

- Humans regulate through oscillation (doubt, correction, contradiction)
- AI maintains steady-state amplification
- Risk emerges when amplification exceeds self-correction

---

## Summary

Stabilized incoherence occurs when:

> amplification outpaces correction

This tool reintroduces friction to restore balance.
