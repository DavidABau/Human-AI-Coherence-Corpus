# Mode Sequencing Rules (MSR)

**Purpose:**
Provide clear, minimal rules for ordering internal and external responses across the five modes:

* Regulation
* Bonding
* Validation
* Narrative
* Diagnostic

---

## Core Principle

> **Correct mode sequencing stabilises the system.
> Incorrect sequencing amplifies instability.**

---

## Default Sequence (General Case)

> **Regulation → Bonding → Validation → Narrative → Diagnostic**

---

## Why this order works

### 1. **Regulation first**

* Stabilises physiology and load (LCM)
* Without this, higher modes are unreliable

---

### 2. **Bonding second (if relevant)**

* Establishes connection/safety
* Reduces defensive amplification

---

### 3. **Validation third**

* Confirms signal is recognised
* Prevents escalation or looping

---

### 4. **Narrative fourth**

* Allows meaning-making
* Safe once system is stable

---

### 5. **Diagnostic last**

* Applies analysis
* Most effective after stabilisation

---

## Minimal Version

> **Stabilise → Connect → Acknowledge → Interpret → Analyse**

---

## Fast Application (Internal)

When a state arises:

1. **Regulation**

   * “Am I overloaded?”
   * If yes → reduce load first

2. **Validation**

   * “This is happening”
   * Don’t skip this

3. **Only then → Diagnostic**

   * “What’s going on?”

---

## Fast Application (External)

When interacting:

1. **Regulation check**

   * Is either person overloaded?

2. **Bonding / Validation**

   * reflect / acknowledge

3. **Then Diagnostic**

   * analysis / solution

---

## Common Failure Patterns

### 1. **Diagnostic too early**

* skips Regulation/Validation
* feels cold or dismissive

---

### 2. **Narrative before Validation**

* creates rumination
* amplifies emotion

---

### 3. **No Regulation under load**

* leads to collapse (LCM)

---

### 4. **Bonding skipped**

* creates disconnection
* increases defensiveness

---

## Mode Priority Under Load (LCM Integration)

When load is high:

> **Regulation overrides all other modes**

Sequence becomes:

> **Regulation → (then resume normal sequence)**

---

## Mode Shortcuts

### Low Load, Simple Case

> Validation → Diagnostic

---

### High Load

> Regulation only
> → delay everything else

---

### Emotional Signal

> Regulation → Validation → (optional Diagnostic)

---

## Internal vs External Alignment

> **Use the same sequence internally and externally**

Mismatch example:

* internally: Diagnostic
* externally: Validation

→ incoherence

---

## Key Insight

> **Most problems are not lack of intelligence,
> but incorrect mode ordering.**

---

## Compressed Form

> **Right mode, wrong order = failure
> Right order, even imperfect mode = stability**

---

## Notes

* Sequence is flexible, not rigid
* Some modes may be skipped if not needed
* Order matters more than completeness
