# Load & Collapse Model (LCM)

**Purpose:**
Explain what happens when system load exceeds the capacity for detection and steering, resulting in loss of control, automatic behaviour, or state lock-in.

---

## Core Principle

> **When load exceeds capacity, detection fails → steering fails → default patterns take over.**

---

## Functional Definition

> **Load = total demand on the system**
> **Capacity = ability to monitor, integrate, and respond**

Collapse occurs when:

> **Load > Capacity**

---

## Components of Load

### 1. Cognitive Load

* problem solving
* multitasking
* decision fatigue

---

### 2. Emotional Load

* unresolved stress
* interpersonal tension
* internal conflict

---

### 3. Physiological Load

* lack of sleep
* illness
* hunger, dehydration

---

### 4. Environmental Load

* noise
* interruptions
* instability

---

## System Capacity

Capacity determines:

* detection sensitivity (SSM)
* integration stability (ECM)
* steering ability (SSM)

Capacity is reduced by:

* fatigue
* prolonged stress
* overload accumulation

---

## Collapse Sequence

```text id="fyb64r"
Increasing Load → Reduced Detection → Missed Steering Window → Automatic Reinforcement → State Lock-In
```

---

## Modes of Collapse

### 1. Reactive Mode

* fast, automatic responses
* minimal reflection

→ “I just reacted”

---

### 2. Narrowed State Space

* fewer perceived options
* binary thinking

→ “this is the only way I feel”

---

### 3. Attractor Dominance

* strongest reinforced pattern takes over

→ default mood/behaviour loop

---

### 4. Temporal Distortion

* time feels compressed or chaotic

→ loss of continuity (links to ECM)

---

## Key Insight

> **Loss of control is not failure of will — it is failure of system conditions.**

---

## Relationship to SSM

* SSM requires:

  * detection
  * selection window

* LCM explains:

  * why that window disappears

---

## Relationship to ECM

* High load disrupts:

  * integration
  * continuity

→ experience becomes fragmented or unstable

---

## Recovery Path (Minimal)

When collapse occurs:

### 1. Reduce Load

* step away
* remove inputs
* simplify environment

---

### 2. Restore Physiology

* breathe
* hydrate
* rest

---

### 3. Re-establish Detection

* don’t force decisions
* wait for signal clarity

---

### 4. Re-enter Steering Window

* small, low-effort actions
* avoid large corrections

---

## Practical Rule

> **You cannot steer effectively under collapse.
> First reduce load, then steer.**

---

## Failure Pattern

Common mistake:

> Attempting high-level control under high load

Result:

* frustration
* further collapse

---

## Integrated Model (ECM + SSM + LCM)

```text id="b6g5v0"
Baseline (Reinforcement)
        ↓
Signal Emerges
        ↓
[IF Load < Capacity]
    → Detection → Steering → Reinforcement Update

[IF Load > Capacity]
    → Detection Failure → Automatic Pattern → Reinforcement of Default
```

---

## Compressed Form

> **Load breaks detection.
> Without detection, there is no steering.
> Without steering, patterns run automatically.**

---

## Notes

* Collapse is normal under high load
* The goal is not to eliminate collapse, but to:

  * detect it earlier
  * reduce its duration
  * recover faster
