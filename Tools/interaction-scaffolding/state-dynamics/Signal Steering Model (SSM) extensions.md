# Signal Steering Model (SSM) — Extensions

## 1. Detection Thresholds

**Purpose:**
Explain why early signal detection is sometimes easy and sometimes almost impossible.

---

### Core Principle

> **Detection depends on signal-to-noise ratio and system load.**

---

### Variables Affecting Detection

#### 1. Baseline Noise

* Mental chatter
* Environmental input
* multitasking

→ High noise = weak signals get buried

---

#### 2. System Load

* fatigue
* stress
* cognitive overload

→ High load = reduced monitoring capacity

---

#### 3. Signal Intensity

* subtle vs strong signals

→ Strong signals bypass detection and go straight to reaction

---

#### 4. Familiarity

* repeated patterns become easier to detect

→ recognition lowers threshold

---

### Detection Equation (informal)

> **Detection ≈ Signal strength / (Noise + Load)**

---

### Practical Implication

> Presence doesn’t “create control” — it **lowers detection threshold**

Which means:

* earlier access to the steering window
* more available trajectories

---

## 2. Reinforcement Loops

**Purpose:**
Explain how temporary states become stable patterns or “default modes.”

---

### Core Principle

> **What is repeatedly attended becomes easier to re-enter.**

---

### Loop Structure

```text
Signal → Attention → Amplification → Reinforcement → Lowered Re-entry Threshold
```

Over time:

```text
State → faster activation → longer duration → stronger baseline
```

---

### Three Reinforcement Channels

#### 1. Cognitive

* repeated thought patterns
* narrative building

#### 2. Physiological

* posture
* breathing patterns
* nervous system tuning

#### 3. Behavioural

* actions that confirm the state
* withdrawal, engagement, avoidance

---

### Result

> The system develops **preferred attractor states**

Examples:

* low mood baseline
* irritability
* calm focus
* anxiety loops

---

## 3. Attractor States

**Definition:**

> **States the system naturally falls into due to prior reinforcement**

Characteristics:

* faster onset
* lower effort to sustain
* bias in perception and interpretation

---

### Key Insight

> You are not choosing states from neutral each time —
> you are selecting from **biased probability distributions**

---

## 4. Steering vs Conditioning

Two different levers:

### Steering (SSM)

* moment-to-moment
* operates in the pre-stabilisation window

### Conditioning (Reinforcement)

* long-term
* shifts which states are easiest to enter

---

### Interaction

```text
Repeated steering → changes reinforcement → alters future steering difficulty
```

---

## 5. Integrated Model

Combine everything:

```text
Baseline (attractor states)
        ↓
Signal emerges
        ↓
Detection (threshold-dependent)
        ↓
Selection (attention)
        ↓
Reinforcement (loop formation)
        ↓
Updated baseline
```

---

## 6. Failure Patterns

### 1. High Load + High Noise

→ no early detection
→ automatic reinforcement of existing attractors

---

### 2. Strong Attractor States

→ narrow steering window
→ rapid stabilisation

---

### 3. Inconsistent Steering

→ no long-term conditioning
→ repeated reset to old baseline

---

## 7. Practical Levers

### Improve Detection

* reduce noise (simplify inputs)
* reduce load (rest, pacing)
* increase familiarity (pattern recognition)

---

### Improve Steering

* act earlier
* use small interventions (don’t wait)

---

### Improve Conditioning

* repeat chosen trajectories
* reinforce desired states consistently

---

## 8. Compressed Form

> **Detection enables steering.
> Steering shapes reinforcement.
> Reinforcement defines baseline.**

---

## 9. Notes

* This model is non-clinical and phenomenological
* Works best for low-to-moderate intensity states
* High-intensity states may bypass detection and require external regulation
