# Signal Steering Model (SSM)

**Purpose:**
Provide a simple, practical method for detecting and redirecting internal states *before* they stabilise into mood, behaviour, or narrative.

---

## Core Principle

> **States arise; attention selects; reinforcement stabilises.**

Or tighter:

> **Early detection → wider steering range**
> **Late detection → higher resistance**

---

## Functional Definition

> **State trajectory is determined at the point of attention, not after stabilisation.**

SSM focuses on the **pre-stabilisation window** — the brief phase where multiple directions remain available.

---

## The Steering Sequence

```text
Signal → Attention → Amplification → Reinforcement → State
```

Where intervention is possible:

```text
Signal → (Attention) → ↑ amplify   OR   ↓ dampen / redirect
```

---

## The Four Phases

### 1. **Signal Emergence**

* Subtle mood shift, sensation, or thought
* Often low intensity, easy to miss

> *Example:* slight heaviness, irritation, drift

---

### 2. **Detection (Presence)**

* Signal is noticed without immediate identification
* Creates separation: *“this is happening” vs “this is me”*

> This is where “being present” actually matters

---

### 3. **Selection (Attention Direction)**

* Attention is applied deliberately
* Possible actions:

  * stay with it (amplify)
  * soften it (dampen)
  * shift away (redirect)

---

### 4. **Stabilisation**

* Signal becomes self-reinforcing
* Narrative + physiology + behaviour align

> After this point, change requires more effort

---

## Key Insight

> **Attention is not passive — it is a selection mechanism.**

Where attention goes:

* signal strength increases
* probability of stabilisation increases

---

## Practical Use (Minimal Protocol)

When a state arises:

1. **Notice early**

   * “Something is shifting”

2. **Name it (lightly)**

   * “low mood starting”
   * “tension spike”

3. **Pause amplification**

   * don’t immediately follow the thought chain

4. **Select direction**

   * stay / soften / shift

5. **Apply small action**

   * posture change
   * breath
   * redirect focus
   * change task

---

## Steering Options

| Action    | Effect                    |
| --------- | ------------------------- |
| Attend    | Amplifies signal          |
| Relax     | Reduces intensity         |
| Redirect  | Shifts trajectory         |
| Interrupt | Breaks reinforcement loop |

---

## Failure Modes

### Late Detection

* Signal already reinforced
* Feels like: “I’m already in it”

→ Requires stronger intervention

---

### Full Identification

* Signal becomes identity
* “I am this mood”

→ Steering collapses

---

### Overcorrection

* Aggressive suppression
* rebound or fragmentation

→ instability

## Notes

* SSM does not eliminate states

* It changes **when and how they stabilise**

* Works best at low-to-moderate intensity

* High-intensity states may bypass early detection window
