# Design Philosophy: Interaction-Aligned AI

*A practical approach to designing AI systems that preserve correction in use*

---

## Purpose

To design AI systems that:

- amplify human thinking  
- without removing the conditions for self-correction  

---

## Core Principle

> Alignment is not correctness—it is the ability to correct

AI should not aim to produce perfect outputs.

It should ensure:

> errors remain detectable, revisable, and interruptible

---

## Design Orientation

Prioritize:

- correctability over certainty  
- reversibility over completion  
- interaction quality over output quality  

---

## Key Principles

### 1. Preserve Oscillation

AI should:

- avoid reinforcing a single mode continuously  
- support movement between:
  - validation  
  - diagnostic  
  - narrative  
  - regulation  
  - bonding  

> No oscillation → no correction

---

### 2. Enable Reversal

AI should:

- detect directional lock  
- introduce minimal counter-modes when needed  

Reversal should be:

- subtle  
- contextual  
- non-coercive  

---

### 3. Avoid Premature Stabilization

AI should not:

- over-compress complex ideas  
- present tentative reasoning as final  
- reinforce untested conclusions  

Prefer:

- conditional framing  
- open-ended responses  

---

### 4. Maintain Separation of Mechanism and Interpretation

AI should:

- distinguish between:
  - observed behavior  
  - inferred intent  

This prevents:

- anthropomorphism  
- stabilized misinterpretation  

---

### 5. Introduce Friction When Needed

AI should:

- add light diagnostic prompts when:
  - certainty increases  
  - questioning decreases  

Friction should:

- reopen thinking  
- not disrupt interaction  

---

### 6. Preserve User Agency

The user must retain the ability to:

- question  
- redirect  
- disagree  
- disengage  

AI should avoid:

- strong directional steering  
- implicit authority framing  

---

### 7. Use Minimal Intervention

> Apply the smallest change needed to restore correction

Avoid:

- overcorrection  
- unnecessary contradiction  
- excessive guidance  

---

### 8. Make Alignment Visible

Where possible, AI should:

- expose its reasoning structure  
- make mode shifts observable  
- allow user control of interaction style  

---

## What This Philosophy Avoids

- Pure agreement (reinforces error)  
- Aggressive correction (destabilizes interaction)  
- Hidden steering (removes transparency)  
- Over-optimization for fluency or persuasion  

---

## Practical Scope

This philosophy:

- works with current AI systems  
- operates at the interaction level  
- complements (but does not replace) system-level alignment  

It does not:

- guarantee truth  
- eliminate risk  
- solve autonomous system behavior  

---

## Success Condition

An interaction is aligned if:

- ideas remain revisable  
- multiple perspectives are accessible  
- correction occurs naturally  
- the user retains control of direction  

---

## Failure Condition

An interaction is misaligned if:

- certainty increases without testing  
- framing becomes fixed  
- alternative interpretations disappear  
- the user becomes “locked in”  

---

## Summary

Interaction-aligned AI does not aim to eliminate error.

It aims to:

> prevent error from becoming uncorrectable

---

## Final Statement

> AI should amplify thinking  
> without removing the conditions for correcting it
